What you're joining

A funded programme that takes thirty students from idea to a real product.

InnovateX gives thirty Indian students the money, the workshop, and the audience to finish one hardware product each, built at IIT Delhi's Central Manufacturing Facility and exhibited in September 2026. As a mentor, you help make sure they get there.

Who we're looking for

If you know how hardware really gets made, you can mentor.

We're inviting graduate-level minds in academia who can review engineering work with real depth and give students honest, useful feedback. Just fifteen mentors are selected in total.

  • Masters, PhD and graduate researchers, and early-career faculty, in academia.
  • Real domain depth in a hardware-relevant field.
  • Willing to review and challenge student work across the phases.
  • Ready to give honest, specific feedback that keeps students the authors.

Fields we're especially keen on

  • Mechanical & design
  • Electronics & embedded
  • Robotics & mechatronics
  • Materials
  • Power & energy
  • Sensors & instrumentation
  • Product / industrial design
Your role

You guide the build. You never take it over.

The work, and the credit, stays with the student. Your job is to make their thinking sharper, not to do it for them.

What you do

  • Review the work each student submits
  • Flag errors and unsound assumptions
  • Point them to prior art and literature
  • Suggest directions worth investigating
  • Answer their specific technical questions

What you won't do

  • Design any subsystem for them
  • Hand over your own code or schematics
  • Fabricate or assemble on their behalf
  • Produce layouts or models for them
Across the programme

Where a mentor makes the difference.

InnovateX runs in five phases between June and September 2026. These are the moments where a mentor's eye matters most. Your exact involvement is confirmed when you register.

Train the Trainers Bootcamp · 14–19 June 2026

Selected mentors gather in person at IIT Delhi for a hands-on bootcamp before the build phase begins. Accommodation on campus is provided for the duration.

  1. 01

    Pitch · 10–30 June

    Help judge whether an idea is real, genuinely new, and buildable.

  2. 02

    Design · July

    Sanity-check 3D models, schematics, and PCB layouts before anything is ordered.

  3. 03

    BOM · August

    Pressure-test the bill of materials for cost, feasibility, and a path to scale.

  4. 04

    Build · August → September

    Answer technical questions as students assemble, debug, and calibrate.

  5. 05

    Exhibition · Mid-September 2026

    The students you guided present their finished units at the InnovateX exhibition.
