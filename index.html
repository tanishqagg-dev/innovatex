What InnovateX is

The funding, the workshop, and the audience so you can actually finish.

We remove the three things that kill student hardware: money, machines, and a reason to ship. We keep the work yours.

  1. 01

    You design a product, not a demo

    Every project names a problem, a user, a form factor, and a target unit cost. Not just a working prototype.

  2. 02

    Hardware leads; software supports

    The core deliverable is physical. Firmware and ML are welcome only where they serve the hardware.

  3. 03

    The capability stays with you

    Mentors review and challenge your work. Bounded scope and timelapse keep you the author of it.

Where you build it

Made at IIT Delhi's Central Manufacturing Facility.

The CMF is the workshop most student projects never get near. IIT Delhi's central facility for precision fabrication and manufacturing. As a participant, you get supervised access to it.

  • Precision machining & fabrication

    Take a rough prototype to a finished, repeatable unit.

  • Supervised access, when you need it

  • Technical review from CMF engineers

    Expert eyes catch errors before they cost you a build.

What makes the cut

We back ideas that are genuinely new.

Not another version of something that already exists. The strongest pitches solve a real problem with a unique edge: a fraction of the cost of what's out there, or tech that simply hasn't been done. Every project is built from these four.

01

A real problem

A specific pain for a specific user. Not a demo looking for a use.

02

A unique edge

Genuinely new: a fraction of the cost, or tech that hasn't shipped.

03

A form factor

A defined physical product with a real shape and footprint.

04

A path to scale

A unit cost that holds up well past the first build.

Authorship & mentorship

Mentors guide you. They never build it for you.

Mentor scope is bounded by design, so the capability and the product stay with you.

What mentors do

  • Review the work you submit
  • Flag errors and unsound assumptions
  • Point you to prior art and literature
  • Suggest directions to investigate
  • Answer your specific technical questions

What they will not do

  • Design any subsystem for you
  • Hand you their own code or schematics
  • Fabricate or assemble on your behalf
  • Produce layouts or models for you
